About

This project seeks to investigate the complex correlation between the Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) categories of each Indian state and its associated Gross Domestic Product (GDP), considering factors such as total food grain production, agricultural output, net irrigated area, and industrial activities across several years.

At Econometrics You will recognize the critical need to optimize resource allocation for better economic outcomes while promoting sustainable development and smart urban planning. Join us on this journey as we explore the intersection of land use, economic development, and sustainable practices in India.

Vision: To create a sustainable future by unraveling the intricate relationship between land use and economic growth, shaping smarter policies and practices for India's development.

PREDICT TOTAL GDP

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is the monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country's borders over a given period, usually annually or quarterly. It's calculated by adding up consumer spending, investments, government spending, and net exports (exports minus imports). GDP serves as a key indicator of a country's economic health and growth, influencing policy decisions and providing insights into overall economic performance.

PREDICT GDP DUE TO AGRICULTURE

To calculate the GDP contribution from agriculture, add the value of all agricultural goods and services produced within a country. This includes the value of crops, livestock, forestry, and fisheries. Additionally, consider the value added by processing and marketing agricultural products. Subtract any costs incurred in production. The resulting figure reflects the contribution of agriculture to GDP, providing insight into the agricultural sector's economic significance.

PREDICT GDP DUE TO INDUSTRY

To calculate the GDP contribution from the industrial sector, sum up the value of goods produced by industries like manufacturing, mining, construction, and utilities. Include the value added at each stage of production, from raw materials to finished goods. Subtract any costs incurred in production, including intermediate goods and services. The resulting figure represents the industrial sector's contribution to GDP, reflecting its importance in the economy.

PREDICT GDP DUE TO SERVICES

To calculate the GDP contribution from the services sector, sum up the value of all services provided within a country. This includes professional services, financial services, transportation, retail, healthcare, education, and hospitality. Consider the fees, charges, and payments for services rendered. Subtract any costs associated with service provision. The resulting figure represents the contribution of the services sector to GDP, reflecting its role as a major driver of economic activity.
